The Data Behind the Work

I'm completing a Master of Science in Data Science at Bellevue University (DSC680 Applied Data Science, May 2026), with a portfolio focused on health outcomes modeling, clinical informatics, and public policy analytics. My work lives at the intersection of machine learning and real-world impact — finding the signal in survey data, clinical records, and policy datasets that can move the needle on decisions that matter.

What sets my background apart is the depth I bring from two complementary fields. As a Registered Nurse with a B.S. in Nursing, I have hands-on clinical experience and have led public health projects in a supervisory capacity — which means I know where data models succeed or quietly fail in practice, and how to communicate findings to clinical audiences who need to trust what they're seeing.

As an AWS-certified Cloud Engineer currently working in production infrastructure, I design and maintain scalable data pipelines using S3, EC2, Lambda, RDS, Redshift, and Glue. I manage environments with IAM, CloudWatch, and CloudFormation — which means I don't just build models; I can architect, secure, and deploy the infrastructure they run on.

That combination — graduate-level data science training, clinical domain knowledge, and production cloud engineering — means I can own the entire lifecycle, from raw data and SQL schemas to a deployed API or a two-page policy brief for legislators.

About

I'm a data science student in Bellevue University's DSC680 Applied Data Science program, building a portfolio that bridges the gap between technical rigor and real-world impact. My work clusters around two domains I care about: workplace mental health and public health policy — areas where the right analysis can actually change how organizations and governments make decisions.

The five completed projects in this portfolio represent genuine problem-solving, not toy examples. I've worked with clinical data from 400 patients, survey data from 3,000 professionals, a decade of childcare pricing across nearly 2,000 U.S. counties, and global demographic datasets that required real data engineering to join together cleanly.

The five planned projects aren't padding — they're filling specific gaps I've identified: NLP for unstructured text, a deployed ML API that shows I can take a model the last mile, time series forecasting, production-grade data pipelines, and rigorous A/B testing. Each one opens a different category of job opportunity.

Before this program, I worked closer to the business side of data — which is why I care as much about the policy brief and the stakeholder deck as I do about the model's ROC-AUC score. Good analysis that nobody can act on isn't good analysis.

Burnout Risk Prediction — Workplace Mental Health Classification

Mental Health · ML

Binary classification system predicting employee burnout risk from survey data across 3,000 professionals. Compared five ML models with feature importance analysis and GridSearchCV tuning.

"Random Forest and Gradient Boosting topped performance. Feature importance revealed stress level, work-life balance, and manager support as the top three drivers — which shaped the final business recommendations."

Python SQLite SQLAlchemy FuzzyWuzzy Pandas Seaborn
PROJECT 03 ✓ Complete

Chronic Kidney Disease Classification — Clinical Feature Analysis

Clinical · ML

Classification on the UCI CKD dataset (400 patients, 24 features) with KNN imputation for 38%+ missing data and engineered clinical features including eGFR, blood pressure categories, and albuminuria.

"The eGFR feature I engineered ranked 6th by mutual information — higher than several original clinical measurements. A nice validation of the feature engineering step."

Python UCI ML Repo Scikit-learn KNN Imputer Mutual Information Seaborn
PROJECT 04 ✓ Complete

U.S. Childcare Affordability Crisis — Visualization & Policy Analysis

Policy · Visualization

Analysis of 1,879 U.S. counties (2008–2018) with three deliverables: interactive Tableau dashboard, two-page policy brief for legislators, and eight-slide stakeholder presentation.

"99% of counties exceed the federal 7% affordability benchmark — families pay 16.3% of income on infant care on average. The urban-rural gap was $103/week. Making that land in a policy brief was a different challenge than the code."

Tableau Public Python Power BI Policy Brief
